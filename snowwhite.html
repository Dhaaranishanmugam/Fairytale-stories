Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs

The Story of Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs

The Queen's Jealousy

The story begins with a Queen who is sewing by the window and pricks her finger. As she sees the red drops of blood on the snow, she wishes for a daughter with skin as white as snow, lips as red as blood, and hair as black as ebony. She names her daughter Snow White.

Snow White's Youth

Snow White grows up to be exceptionally beautiful, which causes the Queen great jealousy as her magical mirror reveals Snow White to be fairer than her.

The Queen's Plot

Out of jealousy, the Queen orders a huntsman to take Snow White into the forest and kill her. However, the huntsman cannot bring himself to harm Snow White and lets her go, instead bringing the Queen the heart of a wild boar.

Snow White in the Forest

Lost in the forest, Snow White finds a cottage belonging to seven dwarfs who live there. They agree to let her stay with them in exchange for keeping house for them.

The Queen's Disguise

Learning from her magic mirror that Snow White is still alive, the Queen disguises herself as an old peddler woman and tricks Snow White into eating a poisoned apple. The apple causes Snow White to fall into a death-like sleep.

The Prince's Arrival

A prince, who had heard of Snow White's beauty, learns of her plight and visits her. Overcome by her beauty, he kisses her and breaks the spell of the poisoned apple.

Happy Ending

Snow White wakes up, and the Prince declares his love for her. They decide to marry, and Snow White invites the dwarfs to the wedding, where they all live happily ever after.

Themes and Moral Lessons:

  • Jealousy and Vanity: The Queen's jealousy over Snow White's beauty drives much of the plot.

  • Good vs. Evil: Snow White's innocence and kindness contrast with the Queen's wickedness.

  • Friendship and Kindness: Snow White's relationship with the dwarfs highlights themes of friendship and hospitality.
