The Fairy Tales!

Puss in Boots

The Story of Puss in Boots

The tale of Puss in Boots is a delightful story about a cunning cat who uses his wit and charm to elevate his penniless master to a life of wealth and nobility.


The Underdog Inherits

A poor miller dies, leaving his three sons a meager inheritance. The youngest son receives nothing but a cat, much to his disappointment.

The Cat with the Plan

This is no ordinary feline! The cat, surprisingly, can talk and boasts a clever mind. He convinces his master to get him a pair of boots and a sack.

Gifts for the King

Puss in Boots ventures out, outsmarts rabbits and partridges, and presents them to the king as gifts from his master, whom he invents a grand title for - the Marquis de Carabas.

Encounter by the River

Puss hatches a plan. He has his master bathe by the river and then hides his clothes. When the king and his daughter ride by, Puss cries out that his master, the Marquis, is being robbed and murdered!

A Carriage Ride and Quick Thinking

The king, fooled by Puss's cries, offers help. Puss hides his master and sends him away with some fancy clothes the king provides. Puss then races ahead, warning everyone they meet to say the land belongs to the Marquis de Carabas.

The Ogre's Castle

Puss reaches a magnificent castle and learns it belongs to a fearsome ogre. He tricks the ogre into transforming into a mouse, whom Puss promptly devours.

A Prince is Born

Puss presents the grand castle to the king as belonging to the Marquis. The king, impressed by the supposed Marquis's wealth and nobility, offers his daughter's hand in marriage.

Happily Ever After (almost)

The cat's master marries the princess and becomes a prince. But remember, he started with nothing!

The Moral of the Story

Puss in Boots teaches us that intelligence and resourcefulness can overcome disadvantages. It's a reminder that sometimes, a little bit of cleverness can go a long way.
